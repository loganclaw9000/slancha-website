Slancha Financial Model

18-month runway model for a seed raise. Based on 2026 GPU pricing, realistic customer ramp, and conservative unit economics.

The Ask: $2.5M Seed

Why $2.5M: 18 months of runway to reach $500K ARR (100 paying customers), prove unit economics, and raise a Series A. GPU costs are the dominant expense — we've modeled conservative utilization with reserved H100s on Lambda/CoreWeave at $2.40-$2.99/hr.

Why This Is Conservative

GPU costs are falling fast. H100 reserved pricing dropped from $4.50/hr (2024) to $2.40/hr (2026). B200s are hitting $3.49/hr. Our model uses today's prices — costs will likely be 20-30% lower by month 12.

Routing is free margin. When Slancha routes to external providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, Together), our GPU cost is $0. We pass through the cost at zero markup, but the platform fee ($49-$499/mo) is pure margin. Early on, most traffic will be routed externally — meaning GPU costs are lower than modeled.

Fine-tuning is absurdly cheap. LoRA fine-tuning a 7B model costs $1-5 per job. Even at 100 customers fine-tuning monthly, that's $500/month. It's a rounding error vs team costs.

The compounding advantage. As more customers use Slancha, more task-specific models get fine-tuned. Each fine-tuned model serves multiple customers with similar workloads. GPU cost per customer decreases as the model library grows.

Series A Trigger (Month 15-18)

Raise Series A when: $500K+ ARR, 100+ paying customers, proven unit economics (>50% gross margin), demonstrated fine-tuning cost savings for at least 10 customers. At that point, raise $10-15M to accelerate to $5M ARR and own the category.
